Description

A two-store home Located in SÃ£o Paulo Brazil needed fixing and improvement. All the electrical, water, sewage installations was substituted as well as floors and roofings. Also a lightning protection ground circuit was installed. All the first floor ambients was level- corrected to adapt the open concept kitchen. The wall dividing the right side of the premises to the neighbour was reinforced. The second level was amplified with one more bathroom and closet.

The old bathroom of the second floor was demolished in part to accomodate one more closet, and part of one bedroom was separated to reinstall a bathroom. A roof covering part of the yard was substituted by a steel structure allowing the wall reinforcement, the area amplification and the kitchen openings. The new area integrated with the kitchen and was separated from the yard with sliding grass doors, so to integrate areas when open.

The service area also recieved sliding translucent glass doors. A existing slab over a edicule room had the roof removed, impermeabilized and recieved two access points, one from the new closet and one from a external stairway connecting to the yard. The space under this stairs had the level lowerd and recieved access from the room now turned a home-office. The building is originally made of solid clay bricks, so the exterior down walls were sanded and protected with transparent coating. The front and the yard recieved a grid lattice custom built.
